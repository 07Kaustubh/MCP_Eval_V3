It is invoice week and I am not comfortable letting this June run go out as it reads. It covers the May work, and as I go down the client lines a few of the amounts are not landing where I would expect, though I have not managed to prove what is wrong from the draft on its own. I would sooner hold the run an extra day and bill people correctly than send out figures I cannot stand behind.

Take the draft as a claim to be checked, not a fact. Go account by account and work out what each client genuinely ought to be billed this cycle, and where an amount has nothing solid under it, do not just flag the gap, settle what it should actually be. A handful will check out and a handful will not, and the supporting detail for any one of them is never in a single system, so pull from all of it together, the mail and messages around the close, what we have on record for each engagement, and what the ledger actually shows.

For every line you correct, leave me a short reason I can stand behind if a partner pushes on it. Some of these sit above my level though, so where a line genuinely needs Andrea or Daniel, pass it to them laid out plainly and leave the call to them, while anything that is truly mine you should carry all the way rather than hand back half done. And none of this is to surface outside the firm yet; no client sees any of it until the partners have put their name to the run.
