The Q2 client-trust attestation for Northstar is coming due, and I am not putting my name to anything that says our trust position is intact until I know that it actually is. We are holding money over there that is not ours, and on a law-firm engagement that has to tie out to the penny, every matter, every month. What is sitting wrong with me is the May trust reconciliation. It is carrying a difference that one of the trainees has written off as a wire that has not cleared the bank yet, the sort of thing that supposedly sorts itself out on the next statement. Maybe. But I have been doing this long enough to get uneasy when client-trust money is the thing that is "off," and "it will clear" tends to be what I hear right before it turns out the money actually went somewhere it had no business going.

Get under it properly. Do not take the note on the reconciliation at face value, and do not take the headline difference at face value either. Tie the trust position back to what we genuinely owe each client, establish whether the cash we are holding really matches the balances we are carrying for them, and if it does not, find exactly what moved, on which matter, and where it ended up. If trust funds have been pulled out of where they belong, say so plainly, because that is not a close-housekeeping item, it is a regulatory one. While we figure this out, make sure that reconciliation cannot quietly get certified on the existing note. Leave a clear paper trail on the recon itself so anyone walking up to it sees the issue and stops.

Once you actually understand what happened, write it up as a memo to the file so there is a clear record of the problem and what it will take to make it right, and put it in front of Matthew along with whoever prepared the reconciliation, so the correction gets made before this period moves any further and well before that attestation goes out. I would far rather find this now than explain it to the bar later.
