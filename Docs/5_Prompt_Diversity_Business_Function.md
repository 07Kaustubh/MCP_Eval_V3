# **Diverse Prompts Tips**

---

Brookfield CPAs & Advisors - Task Categories — 10 categories and 42 subcategories. Each subcategory carries an authoring checklist (eligible personas, entities, systems, artifacts, linked scenarios), a worked example in the protagonist’s voice, and inline definitions of every accounting concept it touches. Categories carry a 🎓 CPA-heavy or 🛠 No-CPA-required tag for pipeline routing — informational, not gating; the guide is written for a generalist audience.

Brookfield tasks should reflect real accounting and advisory work — close cycles, partner sign-offs, controls testing, AML threshold reviews, AP escalations, change-order disputes — not just “check the GL and send an email.” When you’re stuck for a starting point, pick a subcategory and build from its worked example.

🎯 Use it to diversify. If all your tasks look the same, come back here. Each category exercises different systems (Oracle GL vs. SAP Subledger vs. BlackLine vs. Records Vault), different entities (Brookfield’s own books vs. Northstar’s lawyer-trust work vs. Acme’s SaaS close), and different kinds of agent reasoning (the matching principle, FinCEN wire-flag thresholds, OOS absorb-vs-change-order judgement, IOLTA-trust roll-forward, GAAP-improper-request refusal). Your tasks should span that range over time, not cluster on one pattern.

⚡ Follow the patterns, not just the examples. The worked examples in each subcategory are illustrations, not templates to copy. Use them to understand what a rich, multi-action task looks like in this domain — then build your own from the authoring-checklist anchors and what you find in the live universe data.
