# **Diverse Prompts Tips**

---

MoveOps — Task Categories — 6 categories and 19 subcategories. Each subcategory carries an authoring checklist (eligible personas, entities, systems, artifacts, linked scenarios), a worked example in the protagonist's voice, and inline definitions of every mortgage concept it touches.

MoveOps tasks should reflect real logistics/moving operations work — job scheduling, fleet coordination, route optimization, compliance reviews, client escalations, vendor disputes, pipeline tracking — not just "check Airtable and send an email." When you're stuck for a starting point, pick a subcategory and build from its worked example.

🎯 Use it to diversify. If all your tasks look the same, come back here. Each category exercises different systems (Airtable vs. Linear vs. CRM vs. Stripe vs. filesystem), different entities (MoveOps's own pipeline vs. client records vs. vendor partnerships vs. warehouse coordination), and different kinds of agent reasoning (scheduling conflict resolution, capacity planning, route cost calculations, SLA breach detection, damage claim analysis, regulatory compliance, fleet utilization accuracy). Your tasks should span that range over time, not cluster on one pattern.

⚡ Follow the patterns, not just the examples. The worked examples in each subcategory are illustrations, not templates to copy. Use them to understand what a rich, multi-action task looks like in this domain — then build your own from the authoring-checklist anchors and what you find in the live universe data.
